title: Two-tier (MLM) referrals
description: Enable two-tier referrals so affiliates earn commissions on sub-affiliates they recruit. Optional, off by default.
---

Two-tier referrals (commonly called MLM) let an affiliate earn a smaller commission on sales generated by sub-affiliates they recruited. Asteris Affiliates includes this in core from Starter — AffiliateWP charges $199/yr extra for the equivalent.

## Enable two-tier

**Settings → Commissions → Two-tier referrals → Enable**.

When you toggle it on, the affiliate signup form gets an optional "Who referred you?" field. New affiliates who fill it in get assigned a **parent affiliate**.

## Set the tier-2 rate

Default tier-2 rate: **Settings → Commissions → Default tier-2 rate** (e.g. 2.5% — typically a fraction of the tier-1 rate).

Override per affiliate: edit an affiliate → **Tier-2 rate (override)**.

## How it pays out

When a tier-2 affiliate (Sarah) generates a sale:

1. Sarah earns the **tier-1 commission** at her configured rate
2. Sarah's parent (Tom) earns the **tier-2 commission** at the configured tier-2 rate
3. Both commissions are credited independently

Tom does **not** need to be active in the sense of making sales; he just needs to be an approved affiliate.

## Caps + safeguards

- **Maximum recursion depth: 2** (hard-coded — no tier-3 / tier-N). MLM regulation in most jurisdictions limits commission depth.
- **Self-referral blocked** — Sarah can't recruit herself.
- **Circular blocked** — A → B → A loops are detected and the second referral is rejected.

## Legal considerations

MLM is legal in most jurisdictions when the primary revenue is from product sales (not from recruitment fees). Asteris Affiliates doesn't charge sub-affiliates to join, so the structure is **single-product affiliate marketing with a referral bonus** — not a pyramid scheme.

Check your local rules. We're not lawyers.

## Reporting

The Reports page shows tier-1 vs tier-2 commissions separately. Top recruiters appear in the **Recruiter leaderboard** widget.

## Disable later

Toggling off **stops new tier-2 commissions** but preserves existing ones. Affiliates already paired keep their relationship in the database; you can clear all relationships via **Tools → Reset MLM relationships** if you want a clean break.
