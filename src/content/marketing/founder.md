# Why I built Asteris Affiliates

I'm Nick Lord. I'm in Sydney, Australia. I run WooCommerce stores, and I built Asteris Affiliates because I got sick of being my own integration tester every Tuesday.

That's the short version. The longer one is below.

---

## Who I am

Small, tight team. One product. One support inbox.

I'm not a big plugin company. I run a small team out of Sydney through an Australian Pty Ltd (My Cosmic Message Pty Ltd t/a Asteris Commerce, ABN 30 652 358 159, **incorporated in 2021**). No VC. No marketing team. No growth hacker, by design. v1.0 is a small team owning every line of code, every support reply, every roadmap call. The team scales further when the product earns it.

I've been building and hosting WooCommerce stores for clients **since 2021** through the same Sydney Pty Ltd. Five years of running real stores at scale. I know what a real store stack looks like at month six, when you've accreted seventeen plugins, three of them are abandoned, and one of them throws a PHP warning every time someone refreshes the cart. Asteris is the plugin-products division of that same five-year-old company, not a fresh startup.

That's the world Asteris was built in.

---

## The problem I ran into

If you've run a WooCommerce store for more than a year, you already know this story. I'll tell it anyway because the maths is worth seeing on the page.

A typical considered-purchase WooCommerce store ends up with something like this:

- Yoast Premium for SEO — $129/yr
- A wishlist plugin (YITH or TI) — around $79-$94/yr
- A PDF invoice plugin — $79/yr
- Variation swatches — $69/yr
- A product filtering plugin — $99/yr
- A request-a-quote plugin — $99/yr
- Trust badges, sequential order numbers, a link shortener, a back-in-stock notifier, a cart abandonment thing, a customer-account-page enhancer…

By the time you tally it up, you're north of $1,500 a year. I checked the numbers on my own store and they were worse than that, because I'd been buying lifetime deals on AppSumo and Mac Heist and forgetting which ones had expired.

The money isn't even the worst part. The worst part is **Tuesday update day**.

YITH was the biggest offender. Every YITH plugin I installed visibly slowed the site down, and they weren't alone — most of the multi-plugin stack I was paying for was built the same way. Heavy admin pages, scripts loading on the front end whether you used the feature or not, queries running on every product page for things that should have been cached or static. I got sick of how they were being built. That's the conflict that pushed me into building this — not one catastrophic break, the slow grinding tax I was paying on every page load, on every store I ran.

Every Tuesday I'd open WP Admin, see the red update badge, and feel my stomach tighten. Updating plugins on a live store is Russian roulette when you have nineteen of them from twelve different vendors, each with their own release cycle, their own JavaScript bundle, their own opinion about how the cart should render.

Support across that many vendors is its own job. Three of them only answer in business hours that aren't your business hours. Two of them have a forum where the last reply was 2019. One of them got acquired and the new owner doesn't reply at all.

I wanted one plugin. So I built it.

---

## What Asteris Affiliates is

One WordPress plugin. 21 modules at v1.0. One settings panel, one update cycle, one licence, one inbox to email when something breaks.

The 21 modules cover what a real store actually needs: SEO, product filtering, variation swatches, PDF invoices and packing slips, trust badges, sequential order numbers, wishlist, request-a-quote, back-in-stock notifications, and more. Six of them are free on WordPress.org as **Asteris Free** — SEO lite, PDF Invoices lite, Variation Swatches lite, Product Filtering lite, Trust Badges (full), and Sequential Order Numbers (full).

The paid version unlocks the rest plus the full versions of the lite modules. Pricing is on the [pricing page](/lifetime); the short version is Starter at $149/yr, Pro at $349/yr (3 sites), Agency at $549/yr (unlimited), and a one-time **Founder at $149/yr locked** capped at 500 buyers.

That's what it is.

## What Asteris Affiliates is **not**

This part matters more than the feature list, so I'm putting it in its own section.

**It's not a page builder.** I'm not competing with Elementor or Bricks. Asteris assumes you already have a theme and a builder you like.

**It's not a payment gateway, a shipping plugin, or a tax engine.** WooCommerce core and the gateway plugins handle that. I'm not going to reinvent Stripe.

**It's not a dropshipping suite.** No AliExpress imports, no print-on-demand integrations, no spinner-mill product feeds. Asteris is built for considered-purchase stores — the kind where you actually care about your product photos and your customer service reply time.

**It's not multisite-ready at v1.0.** It might be later. It isn't now. If you need WP multisite, this isn't the plugin yet.

**It's not finished.** v1.0 ships with 21 modules. The roadmap has more. Some of what's on the roadmap will change because Founders tell me to change it.

I'd rather you walk away on this page than buy and feel misled.

## How it gets built

Lean, weekly, in public.

I ship every Friday. The cadence is locked — even if the release is small, there's a release. The [changelog](/changelog) is the source of truth; I update it the same day code goes out.

The [roadmap](/roadmap) is public and honest. Items that are committed are marked committed. Items that are "maybe" are marked maybe. Things I've deprioritised are still listed with a note saying why, so you don't have to guess whether I forgot.

Fridays are release day. Mondays are Founder-feedback triage.

When a Founder customer sends a bug report or a feature request, it goes into the same queue I work from. I read every one. Not every one gets built — some don't fit the product, some fit but get queued behind higher-impact work — but every one gets a reply explaining which bucket it landed in.

That feedback loop is the actual value of the Founder tier. The discount is nice. The leverage on what Asteris becomes is the real thing.

## Who it's **not** for

- Dropshippers. The product isn't built for your workflow and I'm not going to pretend otherwise.
- Stores that need WP multisite at v1.0. Not yet.
- Anyone who needs a 24/7 support line. Support runs on Sydney hours. The SLA is 1 business day for Pro, Agency, and Founder; 2-3 business days for Starter; and forum-first with email fallback for Free. I try to beat it. I won't promise 24/7 because we can't honestly deliver 24/7 as a small team.
- Anyone who wants a finished product on day one. v1.0 is real and it works, but it's a v1.0, not a v5.0. If that's a dealbreaker, the [14-day refund](/refund-policy) is genuine — you get your money back inside 24 hours, no argument.
