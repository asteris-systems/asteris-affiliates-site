# Yoast SEO Premium pricing in 2026 — what you actually pay

Yoast SEO Premium is **€118.80/yr ($129/yr USD)** per site in 2026. That's the headline number, but the add-ons sit beside it and the per-site licensing changes the maths quickly. This page walks through every tier, what each one unlocks, what's worth paying for, and what the free version still does well.

All prices verified from yoast.com in April 2026. Yoast bills in euros — USD figures shift with FX.

---

## What Yoast SEO actually is

Yoast SEO is a WordPress plugin from Yoast BV (Wijchen, Netherlands; founded 2010, acquired by Newfold Digital in 2021). It started as a free plugin and now ships as a Free version on the WordPress.org repository plus four paid add-ons sold through yoast.com. Active installs of the free plugin sit above 10 million, making it the largest SEO plugin on WordPress by install count.

The plugin handles title tags, meta descriptions, XML sitemaps, breadcrumbs, schema, social previews, and on-page readability scoring. The paid tier adds redirect management, multi-keyword optimisation, internal linking suggestions, and integrations.

For WooCommerce specifically, the core SEO plugin handles product titles and descriptions, but the WooCommerce-specific schema (Product, Offer, AggregateRating) and breadcrumb tweaks live in a separate paid add-on. This trips up store owners who buy Premium and discover they still don't have WooCommerce product schema.

## Every Yoast price in 2026

Prices in euros (Yoast's billing currency) with USD approximations at €1 = $1.085, accurate to within 2% as of April 2026. All licences are **annual, per site, auto-renew on**.

| Product | EUR/yr | USD/yr | Per-site |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Yoast SEO Free | €0 | $0 | Unlimited |
| Yoast SEO Premium | €118.80 | $129 | 1 site |
| Yoast WooCommerce SEO | €63.20 | $69 | 1 site |
| Yoast Local SEO | €72.40 | $79 | 1 site |
| Yoast Video SEO | €72.40 | $79 | 1 site |
| Yoast News SEO | €72.40 | $79 | 1 site |
| Yoast SEO bundle (Premium + all 4 add-ons) | €209.10 | $229 | 1 site |

The bundle saves €190 over buying everything separately. It only makes sense if you need three or more of the add-ons, which is rare. Most WooCommerce stores need Premium + WooCommerce SEO and nothing else — that combination costs €182 ($198) and the bundle isn't cheaper for them.

### Multi-site discounts

Yoast applies a sliding discount when you licence the same product across multiple sites in one order:

| Sites | Discount on additional sites |
|---|---:|
| 2 | 10% |
| 3-5 | 15% |
| 6-10 | 20% |
| 11-20 | 25% |
| 21+ | 30% |

The discount only applies to the additional sites — site one is full price. Agencies running 10 client sites on Premium pay roughly €1,068/yr ($1,160) for ten Premium licences, not €1,188.

### Renewal pricing

Yoast occasionally runs renewal discounts (typically 20% off the first renewal), but the standard renewal is full price. The €30 "first-year discount" you sometimes see at checkout disappears on renewal.

## Free vs Premium — what changes

The free version is genuinely useful. It's not crippleware. Here's the real split.

### What Yoast Free does

- Meta title and description editing on every post, page, product, taxonomy term
- XML sitemap generation (posts, pages, products, custom post types)
- Breadcrumb output (theme integration required, or shortcode)
- Open Graph + Twitter card tags
- Basic schema (WebSite, Organization, Article, BreadcrumbList)
- Single-keyword content analysis (the green/orange/red bullets)
- Readability analysis (Flesch reading ease, sentence length, passive voice)
- robots.txt and .htaccess editor
- Search Console integration
- Cornerstone content marking (manual)

For a 30-product WooCommerce store with a small blog, this covers everything you actually need to rank. The free version is not the reason small stores struggle on Google — content and backlinks are.

### What Premium adds

The Premium-only features, ranked by how often they matter:

**1. Redirect manager (high value).** Manages 301, 302, 307, 410, and 451 redirects from the WP admin. Automatically creates a redirect when you change a URL or trash a post. Critical for WooCommerce because product URL changes are common — discontinued SKUs, category restructures, brand renames. Without redirect management, every change is a 404, and 404s lose rankings within weeks. Standalone redirect plugins exist (Redirection by John Godley is free and excellent), so this isn't a unique capability — but it's bundled.

**2. Multi-keyword optimisation (medium value).** Lets you target up to five keywords per post instead of one. Useful if you do real keyword research and want one product page to rank for "men's leather wallet" and "RFID blocking wallet". Less useful if you target one keyword per page anyway (which most SEO guidance recommends).

**3. Internal linking suggestions (medium-to-high value for content sites).** As you write a post, Yoast surfaces other posts on your site that contain the same prominent words and suggests them as link targets. Genuinely helpful on a 200+ post blog. Wasted budget on a 12-page brochure site or a thin store.

**4. Social previews (low-to-medium value).** Visual preview of how your Facebook/LinkedIn/X card will look. You can also do this for free with metatags.io or the platform's own debugger, but having it inside WP is convenient.

**5. Content insights (low value).** Word frequency analysis. Pretty graph, rarely changes anything.

**6. Stale content checker (low value).** Flags posts that haven't been updated in 6+ months. A spreadsheet does this too.

**7. Yoast AI title and meta generator (low-to-medium value, beta).** Generates title/meta suggestions from your content. Output quality is mediocre — Claude or ChatGPT direct will write better metas if you're willing to copy-paste.

**8. Zapier integration (low value).** Triggers a Zap on publish. Useful for maybe 5% of users.

**9. 24/7 email support (medium value if you've never used a forum).** Free users get the WordPress.org support forum, which Yoast staff do respond to within a few days.

**10. Yoast SEO Academy access (low value).** Online courses on SEO basics. Free alternatives are everywhere — Ahrefs blog, Search Engine Journal, Moz beginner's guide.

## The WooCommerce SEO add-on

The €63.20 ($69) WooCommerce add-on is separate from Premium and you need both for a complete WooCommerce SEO setup. The add-on provides:

- Product schema (Product, Offer, AggregateRating) — this is the big one. Without it, your products won't show price, availability, or star ratings in Google search results.
- Pinterest Rich Pins
- Better breadcrumb integration on WooCommerce archives
- Title and meta templates specific to product/category pages
- Brand metadata field
- GTIN, MPN, ISBN support for product identifiers

The product schema piece is mandatory if you want rich snippets. If you skip the add-on, you'll need to add product schema another way — Rank Math Free includes it, as does the WooCommerce core (basic version since WC 7.2), or you can hand-roll it.

The honest read: Premium without the WooCommerce add-on is a half-measure for a store. Budget €182 ($198) total or look elsewhere.

## Local SEO, Video SEO, News SEO

These three add-ons are €72.40 ($79) each.

**Local SEO** adds LocalBusiness schema, store locator, opening hours, multiple location support, and Google Maps integration. Worth it for service businesses with a physical location. Not relevant for online-only stores.

**Video SEO** adds VideoObject schema and a video sitemap. Worth it if you host original video content (not embedded YouTube). For 99% of WooCommerce stores, irrelevant — embedded YouTube already gets indexed by Google through YouTube.

**News SEO** adds News sitemap (Google News specific) and publication tags. For publishers approved into Google News only. Irrelevant for ecommerce.

## Per-site licensing — the agency problem

Every Yoast licence is per-site. "Site" means one WordPress install. A multisite network counts as one site only if you activate Yoast network-wide and run a single licence at the network level (this works for Premium but has edge cases on the add-ons).

For agencies running client sites, this gets expensive. Ten clients each needing Premium + WooCommerce SEO = €1,820 ($1,980) per year before any multi-site discount. With the 20% agency discount on additional sites, you pay roughly €1,490 ($1,620). And you renew that annually.

There's no agency tier with bulk-included sites. Yoast Care plans (managed support) exist but don't bundle licences.

## When Premium is worth the money

Buy Premium when at least two of these are true:

- You publish 4+ blog posts per month and have 50+ posts already (internal linking suggestions earn their keep)
- You change product URLs more than once a quarter (redirect manager pays for itself)
- You target multiple keywords per product page deliberately (multi-keyword)
- You don't already have a redirect plugin and don't want a second plugin
- You're an agency billing the licence through to the client

Buy Premium + WooCommerce SEO when you also need product schema and your theme doesn't already output it.

## When Free is enough

Stick with Free when:

- You have fewer than 30 products and fewer than 20 posts
- Your product URLs are stable
- Your theme already outputs WooCommerce product schema (most modern themes do — check view-source for `application/ld+json`)
- You manage redirects through a host-level rule or a free plugin like Redirection
- You're a hobby store or pre-launch

The free version is not the bottleneck. Content, site speed, and backlinks are.

## FAQ

### How much is Yoast SEO Premium in 2026?

€118.80/yr ($129 USD/yr) for one site, billed annually with auto-renewal. The bundle including Premium plus the four add-ons (WooCommerce, Local, Video, News) is €209.10/yr ($229).

### Is the WooCommerce add-on included in Premium?

No. Yoast WooCommerce SEO is a separate €63.20 ($69/yr) add-on. Premium alone doesn't include product schema. For a complete WooCommerce SEO setup with Yoast, budget €182 ($198) per year.

### Does Yoast offer a Founder pricing?

No. All Yoast products are annual subscriptions. They've never offered lifetime pricing and there's no public indication they will.

### What happens if I don't renew?

The plugin keeps working but stops receiving updates and you lose support access. Premium-only features continue to function on your existing version, but you won't get WordPress compatibility updates, security patches, or new features. After 12-18 months this becomes a real risk.

### Can I use one Premium licence on multiple sites?

No. Each licence is per-site. WordPress multisite networks count as one licence at the network level, but separate WordPress installs each need their own licence. Multi-site discounts apply (10-30% off additional sites in the same order), but the discount is on the additional sites, not site one.

### Yoast vs Rank Math vs SEOPress — quick price comparison?

Rank Math Pro is $84/yr (3 sites) or $209/yr (unlimited). SEOPress Pro is $49/yr (unlimited sites). Yoast Premium is $129/yr per site. Yoast is the most expensive on a per-site basis but has the largest installed base and the most polished UX.

### Will Premium make my store rank higher?

By itself, no. SEO plugins handle on-page mechanics — they don't write your content or earn your backlinks. Premium's value is workflow (redirects, internal linking suggestions, multi-keyword) not rankings.

### Do I need Premium if I use Google Search Console?

GSC is free and gives you ranking, indexing, and click data. Yoast Premium doesn't replicate that. They serve different purposes — GSC tells you how Google sees your site, Yoast helps you change what Google sees.

### Can I migrate away from Yoast later without losing my meta data?

Yes, but it requires care. Yoast stores meta titles, descriptions, focus keywords, and redirects in its own database fields. Most competitors (Rank Math, SEOPress, Asteris) include a Yoast import tool that copies these fields over. Always run the import on a staging site first and verify redirects survive.

### Is the AI title generator worth it?

The output is acceptable but not better than asking Claude or ChatGPT directly. If you already use a general LLM, the in-WP AI isn't a reason to buy Premium. If you don't, it's a small convenience.
