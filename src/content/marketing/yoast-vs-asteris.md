# Yoast vs Asteris Affiliates — which is right for your store?

Yoast SEO is excellent at SEO. If SEO is the only thing your store needs, Yoast is the right tool. Asteris Affiliates is for stores that need SEO plus 19 other things — wishlist, side cart, PDF invoices, product filtering, coupons, and more.

That's the comparison. Everything below unpacks it.

The case is bigger than just "more modules."

## What Yoast SEO Premium costs in 2026

Yoast SEO Premium is €118.80/yr per site — approximately $129/yr USD at current exchange rates, verified April 2026.

That's for SEO only: meta titles, descriptions, redirects, internal linking suggestions, and multi-keyword support.

If you also use the Yoast WooCommerce SEO add-on (for product schema, breadcrumbs on WooCommerce pages, and other WC-specific features), that's an additional $69/yr. Combined: approximately $198/yr for Yoast's WooCommerce SEO stack.

---

## What Rank Math costs in 2026

Rank Math PRO is $59/yr for a single site. Rank Math BUSINESS is $199/yr. Both include schema, redirects, advanced SEO analysis, and WooCommerce-specific features.

Rank Math PRO is the most common alternative to Yoast. At $59/yr it's meaningfully cheaper for similar functionality.

## Where Asteris Affiliates fits

Asteris Affiliates Starter is $149/yr. It includes an SEO module with similar core functionality to Yoast Premium — meta titles, descriptions, breadcrumbs, XML sitemaps, redirects, WooCommerce-specific schema.

The SEO module in Asteris Affiliates is not trying to beat Yoast on depth. Yoast has been building their SEO plugin for fifteen years. The Asteris Affiliates SEO module covers what 80% of WooCommerce stores actually need.

What Asteris Affiliates adds: 18 other modules in the same $149/yr. Wishlist, PDF invoices, variation swatches, side cart, product filtering, back-in-stock notifications, quote request, and 11 more.

## When to stay on Yoast

Yoast SEO Premium is the right choice if:
- SEO is the only tool you need from this comparison
- You rely on Yoast's internal linking suggestions
- You use Yoast's multi-keyword optimisation deeply
- You're happy paying $129/yr for best-in-class SEO tooling

Asteris Affiliates's SEO module won't give you the internal linking suggestions or 15+ years of SEO feature development. If those matter to your workflow, stay on Yoast.

## When to consider Asteris Affiliates

Asteris Affiliates makes sense if:
- You're already paying $129/yr for Yoast plus additional plugins for wishlist, PDF invoices, filtering, etc.
- Your SEO needs are met by core features (meta, schema, redirects, sitemap)
- You'd rather pay one company $149/yr than five companies $500/yr
- You want a migration path that preserves your existing Yoast SEO data

## Frequently asked questions

**Does Asteris Affiliates conflict with Yoast if both are installed?**
No. If Yoast SEO is active, Asteris Affiliates's SEO module automatically disables itself. You can run Yoast alongside the 18 non-SEO Asteris Affiliates modules without conflict.

**Can I run Yoast for SEO and Asteris Affiliates for everything else?**
Yes. Many stores do exactly this during a transition period. Yoast handles SEO; Asteris Affiliates handles wishlist, side cart, PDF invoices, and the rest.

**What happens to my Yoast data if I switch?**
The Asteris Affiliates migration tool imports all your Yoast meta data. Your rankings aren't affected — the meta data moves over intact.

**Is Asteris Affiliates's SEO as good as Yoast's?**
For most WooCommerce stores: yes. For stores that rely on Yoast's internal linking suggestions, AI writing mode, or Yoast Academy: no. Honest answer.
