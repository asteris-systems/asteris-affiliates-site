# FunnelKit vs Asteris Affiliates

FunnelKit (formerly CartFlows) and Asteris Affiliates are not competing products. They do different things, and most stores that need FunnelKit also benefit from Asteris Affiliates.

> 📖 **If you're evaluating Asteris on its own merits:** [8 technical + operational reasons to pick Asteris →](/why-asteris) — smaller installed footprint, modern PHP 8.1+ code, HPOS-verified, 6-theme tested, one update + one vendor + one inbox, properly GPL. FunnelKit handles funnels; Asteris handles everything else around them.

---

## What FunnelKit does

FunnelKit builds checkout funnels: custom checkout pages, order bumps, upsells, downsells, and A/B tests on checkout flows. It's for stores focused on maximising revenue per transaction through funnel optimisation.

FunnelKit is good at what it does. Asteris Affiliates doesn't do any of it.

---

## What Asteris Affiliates does

Asteris Affiliates is a 21-module plugin covering SEO, AI content, PDF invoices, variation swatches, wishlist, side cart, product filtering, and 12 more. It's for stores that want to consolidate their plugin stack.

It doesn't touch checkout funnel logic, order bumps, or upsells.

---

## When you'd use both

A WooCommerce store running FunnelKit for checkout optimisation and Asteris Affiliates for SEO, wishlist, and invoices is a normal and sensible stack. The two plugins co-exist without conflict.

---

## If you were looking for a funnel builder alternative

Asteris Affiliates isn't it. The direct FunnelKit alternatives are CartFlows (its predecessor/fork), WooFunnels, and custom checkout flow tools.

---

## FAQ

### Does Asteris replace FunnelKit entirely?

No. Asteris Affiliates does not build checkout funnels, order bumps, or upsell flows. If you rely on FunnelKit for funnel logic, Asteris is not a replacement.

### Can I use Asteris Side Cart with a FunnelKit checkout?

Yes. The Side Cart module operates on the cart drawer and standard cart page, and hands off to whichever checkout you have configured. FunnelKit's custom checkout takes over at that point without conflict.

### What does FunnelKit do that Asteris doesn't?

Custom checkout pages, order bumps, one-click post-purchase upsells and downsells, A/B testing on funnels, and dedicated cart-recovery automation. None of that is in Asteris Affiliates and none of it is on the roadmap.

### Which is better for cart abandonment recovery?

FunnelKit. It has a dedicated automation engine for abandoned-cart emails and SMS sequences. Asteris does not ship a cart-recovery automation module.

### Do I need both?

Only if you need both jobs done. If you want funnel optimisation plus SEO, wishlist, invoices, swatches, and filtering in one stack, running both is reasonable. If you only need funnels, FunnelKit alone is fine.

### What's the pricing comparison?

They are not directly comparable because the scope differs. FunnelKit's funnel-builder plans typically sit in the low-to-mid hundreds per year per site. Asteris Affiliates is a single annual licence covering 21 modules — see the pricing page for current tiers.

### How do they handle one-click upsells?

FunnelKit handles them natively with post-purchase upsell pages tied into the order. Asteris does not offer one-click upsells. If that is the feature you are buying for, choose FunnelKit.

### Which has better email automation?

FunnelKit, by a wide margin. Its Automations product is built around triggered email and SMS sequences. Asteris does not include marketing automation — transactional email customisation only via the invoice and order modules.
